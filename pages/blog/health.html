Health Program

Home / Health Program

1. Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTDs)

SMAid International has taken up the initiative to combat Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTDs) in Ghana. This is achieved through various means. We educate individuals on NTDs so they can make informed decisions regarding their health. We emphasize the importance of early detection, collaborate with regional and national NTD programs, and implement tangible measures to improve the situation. Our collective objective is to eradicate NTDs from Ghana, ensure access to information, and establish healthcare as a fundamental right. This will contribute to a brighter and healthier future for all.

2. Maternal and Neonatal Health (MnCH)

SMAid International is dedicated to revolutionizing maternal and neonatal health (MnCH) in Ghana, prioritizing the well-being of mothers and their precious newborns. Our program focuses on two critical areas: empowering mothers and ensuring newborn health to reduce maternal mortality and provide a healthier start in life. Our MnCH effort relies heavily on community involvement to close healthcare access gaps and increase awareness via collaboration with local healthcare providers, community leaders, and health workers. We provide communities with the fundamental information they need to ensure healthy pregnancies, safe births, and healthy newborns.

3. Reproductive Health and HIV/AIDS Prevention Advocacy

SMAid International leads the charge in promoting healthier communities and empowered lives through its Reproductive Health and HIV/AIDS Prevention Advocacy programs. This initiative is a resolute commitment to providing individuals with the knowledge, tools, and support required to make informed decisions about their reproductive health and prevent the spread of AIDS. Our program thrives on community engagement, forging partnerships with local organizations, schools, and healthcare centers to create a network of support. Together, we drive sustained change in attitudes, behaviours, and overall health.
